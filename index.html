About

Epidemiological parameter estimates

Throughout the course of an infectious disease outbreak, estimates of epidemiological parameters are required to inform and guide public health interventions designed to mitigate the spread of disease and reduce its impact on affected populations.

Epidemiological parameter estimates underpin our ability to quantify and respond to disease outbreaks that can be used by mathematical models - indispensable tools for understanding, forecasting and mitigating the spread of infectious diseases - to simulate, infer and/or/estimate disease transmission dynamics and determine the potential impact of outbreaks in terms of morbidity, mortality, and geographical spread over time. Available information on parameters can also be summarized to directly inform specific public health interventions such as contact tracing.

Parameters can be estimated from observations using a range of methods, from the very simple to highly complex, and can be reported in numerous ways throughout literature.

Community of Practice

Epidemiological Parameters

Since the launch of the Collaboratory initiative by the WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence in 2022., epidemiological parameter estimates has been endorsed as one of the key areas for convening a global Community of Practice that will interact together to pool resources, share knowledge, and co-create solutions to continuously improve existing practices, methodologies, supporting tools, data availability and quality.

Epidemiological parameters Community of Practice is a global multidisciplinary consortium of mathematical modelers, epidemiologists, public health experts, data scientists and IT experts currently counting 100+ active members with a wide network of subject matter experts and enthusiasts that contribute to the specific aspects of the work of the Community.

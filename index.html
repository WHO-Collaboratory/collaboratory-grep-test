Background

Epidemiological parameter estimates

Throughout the course of an infectious disease outbreak, estimates of epidemiological parameters are essential for guiding public health interventions and informing mathematical models used to understand, forecast, and mitigate disease spread. Epidemiological parameter estimates are quantitative measures that describe key characteristics of disease transmission and progression, such as how quickly an infection spreads, how long individuals remain infectious, or how many people actually die from the disease.

However, these estimates are derived using a wide range of methods, from simple calculations to highly complex approaches and are reported in many different ways. This variability limitis timely and consistent ways of finding and using estimates especially during rapidly evolving outbreaks, thus limiting effective use in modeling and public health decision-making.

Epidemiological Parameters Community of Practice (CoP), set up through the Collaboratory initiative of the WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence, is actively addressing these challenges by pooling resources, sharing knowledge, and co-creating solutions through the GREP Initiative.

Community of Practice

Epidemiological Parameters

Since the launch of the Collaboratory initiative by the World Health Organization Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence in 2022., epidemiological parameters has been endorsed as one of key areas for convening a global Community of Practice that will interact together to pool resources, share knowledge, and co-create solutions to continuously improve existing practices, methodologies, supporting tools, data availability and quality. Learn more...

Epidemiological parameters Community of Practice is a global multidisciplinary consortium of mathematical modelers, epidemiologists, public health experts, data scientists and IT experts currently counting 100+ active members with a wide network of subject matter experts and enthusiasts that contribute to the specific aspects of the work of the Community.

Focus

Global Repository of Epidemiological Parameters (GREP) initiative

Mathematical models for infectious diseases are heavily used to support decision-making. These models require reliable epidemiological parameter estimates - such as incubation periods, serial intervals, reproduction numbers and case fatality ratios - to simulate, infer and/or estimate disease transmission dynamics and evaluate intervention strategies.


There is currently no central living repository of epidemiological parameters that the global modeling community can access, use, and contribute to. Existing epidemiological parameter estimates are mostly accessible through the literature and scattered throughout different sources which are often outdated, contextually opaque, and lack standardised reporting.


The aim of a living global epidemiological parameter repository is to serve as a publicly accessible digital public good to inform and guide public health interventions designed to mitigate the spread of diseases and reduce their impact on affected populations, and to facilitate decision making and enable effective response to public health risks. It would provide a systematic, transparent, and reproducible dataset to better and faster understand heterogeneity and quality in reporting and the context from which epidemiological parameters were derived and save researchers time by streamlining the identification of relevant estimates in the literature. In addition, it would facilitate more informed discussions about parameter selection and use in epidemiological models.


While World Health Organization provides hosting for the repository, it is being collaboratively developed, led and maintained by the community.

Workstreams



Core Technical Working Group


GREP Core Technical Working Group is comprised of subject matter experts who drive definition, design, development and implementation of an initial pilot of a global repository of epidemiological parameters, providing it with curated data and connecting it with existing epi tools.
